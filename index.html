ABOUT

Teaching Philosophy

As a universal language art manifests itself in the world around us in so many ways. I believe developing visual literacy is incredibly important and will help open up a wealth of opportunities to students.

Investigating art is a gateway to understanding the human condition, where we came from, and how we function. Understanding art not only gives students another way of connecting with the world around them, but also helps them to see their world in multiple dimensions and perspectives.

I am particularly interested in the cross-pollination between the traditional definitions of fine arts, handmade crafts, performance, moving image and digital work and aim to help student expand their own definitions of what art can be. I believe it is not what you do in life but how you do it that is important. Art requires a unique way of working and thinking. I hope to open and expose students, from all playing fields, to the diversity of creative expression to be found in any arena of imagination.

My goal is to encourage innovative thinking and an awareness of learning through creative investigation — to provide students with the information, resources, and encouragement to ask the right questions, chase the inspiration, and come up with the big ideas. I want to develop and encourage the importance of creative thought that can then be turned into inspired action.

About Me

By definition I am a TCK (Third Culture Kid). I have lived in and travelled to many places across the globe, but New Zealand is the place I call home. I was born in Scotland and briefly lived in the Caribbean whilst immigrating to New Zealand where my family settled in 1995. My accent battles with dual nationality and I am often mistaken for Canadian, which makes for an interesting conversation point. On top of that I have benefited from being part of an expatriate community in Qatar, Middle East, between 2007 and 2013.

I believe my international experience contributes to my friendly, outgoing, and ambitious personality. I enjoy a challenge and seize every opportunity that crosses my path.

The melting pot of experiences and influences I have enjoyed has also impacted my artistic interests. My eclectic tastes, from the masters of Art History to trends in pop culture, from fine arts, handmade crafts and moving image are shaped by my intrepid exploration of new horizons.

I am passionate about creativity in all of its shapes and forms. Most of all I am passionate about fostering creativity in others. I love teaching and sharing ideas and feel privileged to have been able to do so much of what I love in my career so far.
