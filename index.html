Commission

Want to get a mural? Here's how...

  • Stage 1

    Get in touch

    Request a mural package with details regarding our engagement process, pricing and a questionnaire about your project

  • Stage 2

    Free Consultation

    We will arrange a free consultation (on site if possible) to discuss ideas, timeline requirements and pricing. From this we will supply you with a commission agreement, which includes a price quote for design and painting services

  • Stage 3

    Design

    We will provide you with a digital design of your mural, also shown insitu for your reference. The design price includes up to two revisions if required. Once design is approved we can start painting! Note: Payment for design must be made in advance and is non-refundable

  • Stage 4

    Painting

    We take the upmost care to create your mural using high quality materials. Note: Half of the painting payment is required one week in advance of work starting while the remaining half must be paid within one week of the work being completed.

In Our Hands

Blake Street Carpark, Rangiora

In early 2021 we learnt a lot about 'Going Big or Going Home' on this monumental project (150 square meters!) in the Blake Street carpark in Rangiora. It was (and still is) a labour of love that has seen a lot of blood sweat and tears but has ultimately been an absolute passion project. We love this mural!

This work - titled 'In Our Hands' - was part of a community project with NEST Arts Collective, the result of winning a Keep New Zealand Beautiful nature mural competition and supported by a Waimakariri Creative Communities grant. There were a lot of hiccups as we navigated the carpark, big scaffold equipment, temperamental weather and a shifting timeline. But positive comments and support from people passing by encouraged us and it has been so rewarding to hear from people who love the burst of colour in the community.

We believe murals, public artworks, visual messages and colour add so much to the texture and personality of a place. We really hope to bring more beautiful murals to our local spaces!
