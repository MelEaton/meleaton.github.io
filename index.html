About Mel

D.O.B - 22/10/1982

I have lived in and travelled to many places across the globe, but New Zealand is the place I call home. I was born in Scotland and briefly lived in the Caribbean whilst immigrating to New Zealand where my family settled in 1995. My accent battles with dual nationality and I am often mistaken for Canadian, which makes for an interesting conversation point. On top of that I benefited from being part of an expatriate community in Qatar, Middle East, for close to eight years.

I believe my international experience contributes to my friendly, outgoing, and ambitious personality. I enjoy a challenge and seize every opportunity that crosses my path. The melting pot of experiences and influences I have enjoyed has also impacted my creative interests. My eclectic tastes, from the masters of Art History to trends in pop culture, Indie Rock to Classical Opera, from fine arts to handmade crafts are all shaped by my intrepid exploration of new horizons.

Film and Media Production

In this subject students produced video work for a range of purposes and contexts - promotional videos for the school, short films and video art installation inspired by artists like Bill Viola and Tony Oursler. The aim of the course is to develop both technical skills - such as camera control, editing skills, sound engineering and lighting, as well as scripting, storyboarding and planning/managing a production project - and conceptual understanding of film by looking at a wide variety of artist models and examples.
I introduced the Youth International Silent Film Festival competition to my classes and was very proud to see not only 3 groups rank in the top 10 but two of our girls win second place in the 2016 competition. They were invited to the International festival in Portland and visited America for the festival and workshops in June, 2017. Since then one of these girls has gone onto take first place in the 2017 competition.

Classes I taught were:

  • Year 9 and 10 Nga Toi
  • Level 1 and 2 Contemporary Maori Art (Painting)
  • Level 2 and 3 Film and Media Production

Inspiration Station

June 2013 - April 2014

Doha, Qatar

I founded a multi-function Art and Craft centre in Doha, Qatar offering a wide range of creative activities and classes for adults and children. Inspiration Station opened in September 2013 and rapidly grew in popularity. To see it flourish was hugely rewarding.
I believe my work managing Inspiration Station has extended a variety of skills that make me an extremely valuable asset to a school and community.

My role as manager included:

  • Design and creation of space – I was in charge of designing the allocation of space as well as furnishing to create a functional and creative facility.
  • Marketing – I employed targeted marketing (such as developing contacts with newspapers and magazines, promoting with local schools and community groups and networking and marketing online) as well as creating a distinct brand identity.
  • Graphic designer – I extended my graphic skills and familiarity with Adobe software when creating all of our branding and promotion material.
  • Social media and online presence – As part of our marketing I set up and maintained various social network accounts and built our website.
  • Events – I organised and hosted a number of events at Inspiration Station, from custom birthday parties to larger handcraft markets.
  • Administration – I set up an online registration system for all of our classes and managed all administration for the centre, including a calendar of events, emailing/phoning customers, promotions and more.
  • Managing budget – I maintained the centre’s budget when purchasing materials, taking class fees etc.
  • Sourcing and managing resources – Obtaining materials in Qatar is easier said than done. Searching, negotiation and ingenuity is involved when stocking for class activities.
  • Creating and teaching lessons – I thoroughly enjoyed creating exciting and rewarding lesson plans for a range of ages, abilities and interests.
  • Managing staff – As the business grew I added and managed more tutors.

Building a Community

Head of Secondary Art and Design

Park House English School

September 2017 - June 2013

Doha, Qatar

Park House is a British curriculum co-education international school with a student body of children from countries all around the globe.
At the time of my employment a new purpose built Art facility was finishing construction and I was tasked with establishing the art department. The secondary school had been running for a few years at that point and was just about to launch it's first year 13 cohort. When I started there were no curriculum resources for the department and tools and materials were very limited. In effect I was working with a blank slate. I am proud of the journey that the department took in the six years I was HOD and the legacy I was able to leave.

Key responsibilities included:

  • Curriculum development – Created dynamic schemes of work and lesson plans for the department that could cater to students from years 7-13.
  • Leadership – Provided valued support, guidance and training to staff  within the department as well as acting as disciplinary and subject leader to students.
  • Budget holder – Maintained budget for annual order, ‘big ticket’ items and general  department costs throughout the year. This task often required innovative resourcefulness  as materials were often not available in Qatar and substitutes would need to be found.
  • Varied assessment – Within senior examination I have experience with the Cambridge exam board, which requires shipping work to UK for assessment of an externally set question. I then facilitated the transition from Cambridge examination to Edexcel, which consisted of an internally set portfolio of work and a visiting moderator. Within junior assessment I worked closely with staff to moderate work and ensure consistent grading of student progress based on criteria that we created together in line with the  National Curriculum.
  • Informed Reporting – I ensured that school policy and department guidelines for  reporting were followed consistently. Also that parents and students were provided  with quality and quantitative information regarding their progress.
  • Differentiated learning – Due to the international nature of the school I would teach groups of students from diverse languages, academic backgrounds and levels of ability. It was important to make sure work issued was clear in delivery and would allow  all students to develop their skills.
  • Strong subject knowledge – Experienced in delivering lessons on a wide variety of  technical skills (printmaking techniques, 3D construction, textile design etc.); art theory (elements and principles of design, colour theory etc.); and subject content (art history, art appreciation).
  • Proficient with ICT – I am familiar with, and frequently use, Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator and InDesign in my teaching. I am also confident with Excel and Word for other administration work. I am also qualified in web development.
  • Innovative lesson delivery – I pride myself on employing a variety of techniques for delivering lessons that stretch student’s abilities and confidence. Discussions and debates, group activities and challenges, experimentation and competition all help extend the creative process beyond just ‘making things’.

CLasses I taught were:

  • British Curriculum Key Stage 3 Art and Design Technology (Year 7 to 9)
  • IGCSE Art and Design (Year 10 and 11)
  • AS Level Art and Design (Year 12)
  • A Level Art and Design (Year 13)
  • AS Level Photography (Year 12)

Saint Kentigern College

January 2006 - December 2006

Auckland, NZ

SKC was my first teaching placement and definitely provided me with a strong foundation and confidence in my teaching.
At SKC, where each student is issued a laptop (at the time a somewhat special characteristic of the school), I learned to make best use of new technologies available to better teach my students and manage resources. This experience certainly set the tone for the rest of my teaching career and I have always sought to make sure I make best use of the technologies available.

My experience at SKC has helped me in all of my subsequent teaching positions by providing a reminder that teaching values is as important as teaching subject content.
