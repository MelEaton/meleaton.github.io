Commission

Want to get a mural? Here's how...

  • Stage 1

    Get in touch

    Check out our mural package with details regarding our engagement process and fill in our questionnaire about your project so we can start talking about bringing your ideas to life

  • Stage 2

    Free Consultation

    We will arrange a free consultation (on site if possible) to discuss ideas, timeline requirements and pricing. From this we will supply you with a commission agreement, which includes a price quote for design and painting services

  • Stage 3

    Design

    We will provide you with a digital design of your mural, also shown insitu for your reference. The design price includes up to two revisions if required. Once design is approved we can start painting! Note: Payment for design must be made in advance and is non-refundable

  • Stage 4

    Painting

    We take the upmost care to create your mural using high quality materials. Note: Half of the painting payment is required one week in advance of work starting while the remaining half must be paid within one week of the work being completed.

In Our Hands

Blake Street Carpark, Rangiora

Where 'Off The Wall' Began...

In early 2021 we learnt a lot about 'Going Big or Going Home' on this monumental project (150 square meters!) in the Blake Street carpark in Rangiora. It was a labour of love that has seen a lot of blood sweat and tears but has ultimately been an absolute passion project. We love this mural!

This work - titled 'In Our Hands' - was part of a community project with NEST Arts Collective, the result of winning a Keep New Zealand Beautiful nature mural competition and supported by a Waimakariri Creative Communities grant. There were a lot of hiccups as we navigated the carpark, big scaffold equipment, temperamental weather and a shifting timeline. But positive comments and support from people passing by encouraged us and it has been so rewarding to hear from people who love the burst of colour in the community.

We believe murals, public artworks, visual messages and colour add so much to the texture and personality of a place. We really hope to bring more beautiful murals to our local spaces!

#YouRock

Katie Riddell Gym, Rangiora

Getting Motivated

Katie wanted a graffiti style inspirational message for her home-based gym. She was fully renovating her garage and wanted the walls to go from drab conrete blocks to something fantastic. As she trained mostly female customers she wanted something appropriate without being too girly. We settled on a quote and design that was a lot of fun to create and right on brand for her personal training gym!

Gamer Heaven

Ten Forward, Rangiora

Let's Play!

When we met Ten Forward they were a brand new business looking for bold, fun and funky artwork for their walls. As a gaming center, that would offer afterschool activities and birthday party functions, we knew we obviously need to draw our inspiration from the games that kids would be playing in the space. For this commission we wanted to create an imersive experience. We evaluated the space and worked back and forth with Ten Forward to design how the mural could make maximum impact whilst staying within their budget for a new business. The results have been a hit with everyone that steps through the door.

We also put together the design for window decals that were on brand and worked well with the overall design of the location. We liaised with a local printer to deliver the designs which they intalled.

Cabinet Art

Chorus Commission

The Chorus Cabinet Art program is an awesome opportunity offered to artists, asking them to submit designs for revamping the many telecommunications cabinets across New Zealand. Once a year a selection of cabinets are made available for painting and working in partnership with local councils they choose from designs submitted and commission local artists to get to work. We made our mark on the cabinet on High Street, Rangiora and we hope to have a go at more in the future!

Wild In Art

Pop Up Penguin Art Trail

Waddle Waddle!

There have been several Wild In Art trails in New Zealand, a brilliant charity fundraising event. We were increadibly excited to be part of the Pop Up Penguin art trail in Christchurch. Artists from all across the city submitted designs for consideration and those who were chosen by sponsors were commissioned to paint large fibreglass sculptures. It was an amazing experience from start to finish, from collecting our sculptures for painting, meeting other artists participating in the trail, exploring the city to discover painted penguins and attending a nailbiting auction where over one million dollars was raised for charity.

Between us we painted 3 penguins - Birds of a Feather, Ice and Punk It Penguin.
