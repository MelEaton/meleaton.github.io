MY WORLD

I live in beautiful Waimakariri/North Canterbury with my lovely little family - my partner Tim, our son Fox and Tim's children (my bonus kids) Will, Penny and Josh. We love to take full advantage of the beaches and mountains around us.

I have lived in and travelled to many places across the globe, but New Zealand is the place I call home. I was born in Scotland and briefly lived in the Caribbean whilst immigrating to New Zealand where my family settled in 1995. My accent battles with dual nationality and I am often mistaken for Canadian, which makes for an interesting conversation point. On top of that I benefited from being part of an expatriate community in Qatar, Middle East, where I taught Art for close to eight years.

MY LEARNING

I have always enjoyed learning new skills and exploring new ideas to help me express my creativity. As such I have an eclectic bag of tricks and I'm a bit of a jack-of-all-trades...painter, sculptor, coder, seamstress, builder, baker...candlestick maker?

I believe having a diverse range of skills has been a benefit to my teaching of Visual Arts. To help improve my teaching practice I am always looking for new skills and understanding that I can use to improve the learning experience for my students.
